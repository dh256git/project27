Reviewing the readability and engagement of existing content

Read the activity description below, browse available tasks, and bid for one you'd like to work on.

Activity description

Is your vocabulary an infinite space? Is your favourite dinosaur a thesaurus? We want our content to be as accessible as possible to learning disabled people, and generally an easy read to all. The notes and posts should also be engaging - not only thought provoking, and educational. We like short sentences, and dislike spelling mistakes. If you have a good eye for catching missing punctuations, and a talent to cheer written content up, please consider becoming a reviewer for readability and engagement.

Who am I looking for?

Somebody, who:

  • pays attention to detail, and is good with words;
  • is able to review written text, and suggest improvements to its readability;
  • has a skill to turn a fact sheet into an engaging work of art, using the right vocabulary.

Browse available tasks

To browse tasks, open our public task board on Trello.

Once you found a task you like the sound of, go ahead and bid for the task using the form below.

Pro tip: Filter tasks using the label names, such as "#ResearchAndDevelopment" to find tasks relevant to specific teams.

Bid for tasks

Fill in your name, Trello username, card link, and e-mail address fields in the form below. We aim to get everyone assigned to their chosen task within 24 hours. In the meantime, check out the additional disclaimers.

Can't find a suitable task? Create your own!

If there is nothing you like the sound of on our task board, we invite you to propose a new task, that you would be happy to take on.

You can do this in 5 quick and simple steps:

  1. Open a new e-mail, using the link above. The "To" field will already have our e-mail address in it.
  2. Type the name of the proposed task into the subject field of the e-mail. Come up with something short, but descriptive. 6-7 words should be enough.
  3. Type the description of the task in the body of the e-mail. Try to keep it short (around 50 words), and answer these questions:
    • What do you plan to do? For example, fixing a bug, or implementing a new feature.
    • Why is this useful for the project? For example, what problem does it solve?
    • What do you expect to learn from the task?
    • How long do you think it will take you to complete the task? For example, 8 hours.
  4. Send the e-mail, then check the task board. The task you proposed should be in the "new tasks" list.
  5. Finally, bid for the task you just proposed, using the bidding form above.

Disclaimers on the bidding process:

  • A Trello account is required to bit for tasks. It is free and simple to register. Trello has excellent accessibility too, so we highly recommend experiencing it.
  • Once we add you as a member to the task board, and assign a card to you, a notification will be sent by Trello. Keep an eye on it.
  • We ask for your email address, such that we can get in touch, if we have a question with regards to your bid. Sharing your e-mail address is optional, but we highly recommend it, so we can reach out to you.
